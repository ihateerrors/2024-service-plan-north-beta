Lynnwood Link Extension

The Lynnwood Link Extension project will extend light rail from Northgate into Snohomish County, serving four new stations: Shoreline South/148th, Shoreline North/185th, Mountlake Terrace and Lynnwood City Center. An infill station will open at NE 130th Street in 2026. The Lynnwood Link Extension project has progressed on schedule and could be ready to open as soon as summer/fall 2024. 

When Lynnwood Link Extension opens in 2024, trains will initially run less frequently than previously planned because it will initially operate as an extension of the current 1 Line only. Trains will run as often as every eight minutes during rush hour and 10 minutes midday, evenings and weekends. The frequency will be every 15 minutes in the late evening. Until the I-90 segment of East Link Extension opens and trains can cross Lake Washington to access our Operations and Maintenance Facility (OMF) East in Bellevue, service to Lynnwood will be less frequent due to the limited storage capacity of our Central OMF in Seattle. This second OMF in Bellevue is needed for the additional storage and maintenance space for our expanding light rail network to ensure our light rail cars are serviced and tested for frequent use. We’re continuing to explore creative solutions for train storage and OMF East access in order to run trains as frequently as we can on Lynnwood Link when it opens next year. 

What you should know:

  • Lynnwood Link Extension is currently scheduled to open in summer/fall 2024.
  • Link light rail will arrive as frequently as every eight minutes on the 1 Line between Angle Lake and Lynnwood.

Revive I-5

The Washington State Department of Transportation has planned dozens of preservation projects to revive I-5. This important freeway was built in the 1960s and needs major preservation and renovation work. Construction projects are happening one section at time over a span of 10 or more years.

ST Express changes

With the Lynnwood Link Extension scheduled to open for service in summer/fall 2024, Sound Transit is proposing changes to Express bus service in the area to connect riders with new Link stations. New Link light rail service provides the opportunity to simplify service with fewer bus routes, add all-day and all-week service, and offer a reliable alternative to the congested I-5 corridor between Lynnwood and Northgate Station. For example, many existing bus routes with a southernmost terminus at Northgate Station will connect to the Link light rail network further north in Lynnwood.

To see how your Express bus route may change, see the proposed changes below.

  • To simplify service, Route 510 will be eliminated and replaced by Route 512 when the Lynnwood Link Extension opens.
  • Route 512 will operate all-day and all-week in both directions, starting from the early morning and continuing until late evening. The introduction of Route 512 will offer expanded service compared to Route 510.
